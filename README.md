## LoMD-UNet: Advancing Underwater Semantic Segmentation through Lightweight Adaptation and Feature Refinement
### Abstract
Underwater semantic segmentation remains a challenging task due to severe image degradation caused by light scattering, color distortion, low contrast, and complex background interference. Although recent SAM-based segmentation frameworks have demonstrated strong semantic representation capability, their direct application to underwater scenes is still limited by insufficient domain adaptation, inadequate preservation of fine-grained structures, and weak modeling of long-range contextual dependencies. To address these challenges, this paper proposes LoMD-UNet, an enhanced SAM2-UNet framework for underwater semantic segmentation. Specifically, a lightweight Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) strategy is introduced into the SAM2 encoder to enable efficient underwater-specific feature adaptation while maintaining the strong representation capability of the pretrained backbone. In addition, a Dynamic Feature Refinement module combining channel and spatial attention mechanisms is designed to strengthen discriminative feature extraction and suppress background interference. Furthermore, a Mamba-enhanced decoder is incorporated to improve long-range dependency modeling and preserve segmentation continuity and boundary integrity. Extensive experiments conducted on the SUIM and DUT-USEG benchmarks demonstrate that the proposed LoMD-UNet consistently outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods across multiple evaluation metrics. In particular, LoMD-UNet achieves 81.44\% mIoU and 89.65\% mF1-score on the SUIM dataset, while obtaining 70.18\% mIoU and 79.72\% mPA on DUT-USEG. Both quantitative and qualitative results verify that the proposed framework effectively improves segmentation accuracy, robustness, and fine-grained boundary perception under challenging underwater environments.
